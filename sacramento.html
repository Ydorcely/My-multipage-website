About Sacramento

Sacramento, the capital city of California, is located in the northern part of the state, in the Central Valley region. As of the 2023 estimates, the city's population is approximately 540,000, making it the sixth-largest city in California.

In terms of income, Sacramento's average income level is slightly below the state average. While the median household income in Sacramento is around $75,000, the statewide median is closer to $84,000. However, Sacramento remains more affordable in terms of housing costs compared to other major California cities. This affordability attracts people from pricier regions, contributing to steady population growth.

State Map

The city is classified as urban, although it has suburban neighborhoods, especially in areas surrounding the core downtown. Sacramento's urban center includes a vibrant cultural scene, government offices, businesses, and educational institutions, but the city’s overall atmosphere is more relaxed compared to larger metropolitan areas like Los Angeles or San Francisco.
