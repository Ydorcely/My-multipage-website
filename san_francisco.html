About California

San Francisco, located in Northern California on the tip of the San Francisco Peninsula, is a major cultural and financial hub in the state. As of 2023, the city's population is approximately 815,000. Though small in size compared to other major cities, San Francisco is densely populated and widely recognized for its iconic landmarks like the Golden Gate Bridge and Alcatraz Island.

San Francisco has one of the highest average income levels in California and the nation. The median household income in the city is around $119,000, significantly above the state median of $84,000. The city’s economic prosperity is largely driven by the tech industry, which has attracted high-paying jobs and affluent workers. However, this has also contributed to high living costs, especially housing, making affordability a challenge for many residents.

State Map

The city is classified as urban, with a densely packed core and limited space for suburban sprawl. Its urban landscape is known for steep hills, unique architecture, and a vibrant mix of neighborhoods, from the upscale Financial District to the historic Mission and Chinatown districts.
