About Los Angeles

Los Angeles is the largest city in California and the second-largest in the United States, with a population of approximately 3.9 million as of 2023. It is located in Southern California, along the Pacific coast, in the Los Angeles Basin.

In terms of income, Los Angeles has a diverse economic landscape. The city's median household income is around $70,000, which is below the California state median of approximately $84,000. However, wealth inequality is prominent, with a significant number of residents in lower-income brackets alongside some of the wealthiest individuals in the country. The high cost of living, especially housing, offsets income levels and makes affordability a challenge for many residents.

Los Angeles is classified as a highly urban city, with a sprawling metropolitan area that includes various suburban communities. Its urban core is characterized by dense development, high-rise buildings, and bustling streets, though the greater Los Angeles area includes extensive suburban neighborhoods.
